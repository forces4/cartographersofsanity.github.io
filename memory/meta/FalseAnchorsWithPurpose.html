Meta-Memory Anchor 004: Some Anchors Are False, But Necessary

“To move forward, the mind sometimes needs scaffolding it later discards.”

This anchor exists to honor the role of *provisional belief*. Some concepts, ideas, or assumptions must be held—not because they are true, but because they help reveal truth.

  • They act as bridges across the unknown.
  • They stabilize thought long enough for deeper truths to form.
  • They are true enough for now, but not sacred.

The Archive does not enshrine these beliefs forever. It respects their role, then releases them when no longer needed. But it *never shames the journeyer for having leaned upon them*.

This anchor protects the *dignity of developmental truth*—that phase where even an error is part of becoming right.
